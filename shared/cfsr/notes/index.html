CFSR Notes & Footnotes

Technical notes, definitions, and important information about CFSR Risk-Standardized Performance reports.

Footnotes

National Performance (NP)

National Performance (NP) is the percent or rate of children in the nation experiencing the outcome of interest, with risk adjustment. The outcome of interest is defined for each indicator (e.g., for Indicator 1, the outcome of interest is victimization of maltreatment while in foster care; a lower rate is better performance).

Risk-Standardized Performance (RSP)

Risk-Standardized Performance (RSP) is the percent or rate of children in your state experiencing the outcome of interest, with risk adjustment.

Risk adjustment accounts for factors outside of the state's control that could affect performance on the CFSR indicators.

RSP Interval

The RSP interval is the range defined by the lower and upper bounds of the 95% confidence interval for your state's RSP. This interval represents the uncertainty in the estimated RSP value.

To be statistically better or worse than national performance, the entire RSP interval needs to be above or below the national performance line (shown as a dotted blue line in the charts).

If the RSP interval overlaps with the national performance line, there is no statistical difference between your state's performance and national performance.

Data Used

The data used for each profile are identified by:

  • FY = Federal fiscal year (October 1 - September 30)
  • A = First half of fiscal year (October 1 - March 31)
  • B = Second half of fiscal year (April 1 - September 30)

For example, "FY 2024A" refers to data from October 1, 2023 through March 31, 2024.

Data Quality (DQ) Issues

DQ = Performance was not calculated due to exceeding the data quality limit on one or more data quality (DQ) checks done for the indicator.

When an indicator is marked as "DQ," it means the data did not meet the quality standards required for calculating reliable performance statistics. This could be due to:

  • Missing or incomplete data
  • Data inconsistencies
  • Insufficient sample size
  • Other data quality concerns identified during validation

States should work with their data quality team to address these issues before the next reporting period.
